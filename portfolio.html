Campaign: The Year of the Burned Sleep

In the year 1133 P.T., now infamously known as the Year of the Burned Sleep, a terrifying plague swept across the lands of Khorvaguad. Nightmares have become all too real, with those who dare to sleep awakening no more, their bodies found charred as if consumed by an unseen inferno. Fear of sleep now grips the hearts of many, pushing them to seek out magical wards or potent substances to stave off rest. The Merchants Guild, ever opportunistic, has monopolized safety, offering dream-warded inns at an exorbitant cost, making uninterrupted rest a luxury few can afford. Survivors speak of waking within their dreams, surrounded by demons of flame or monstrous, fire-breathing beasts. In desperation, some turn to the Citari Elves’ Dream Powder—a temporary escape from sleep, though it comes at a cost. As the nightmares worsen, no one knows how long this cursed year will last or how many more lives it will claim.

Nation's of Khorvaguad

  • Kingdom of Fithe:
    Once a mighty force of arcane explorers, the Kingdom is now a shadow of its former self, relying heavily on the druids of Essos Grove for survival. Their famed city, Port Orling, remains a beacon of maritime trade and arcane research.
  • Holy Rhalm Empire:
    A crumbling empire that once ruled with an iron fist, now weighed down by political alliances with the Khor and Citari Elves. Cities like Weaver’s Town are controlled by merchant guilds, and the beastfolk inhabit Lo'Kara.
  • Rovania:
    A land once known for barbaric raiders, it now houses the prestigious Vordir Academy for arcane study. Its rugged people—humans, orcs, and goliaths—have evolved into disciplined scholars and warriors.
  • Federation of Khor:
    This powerful maritime nation, ruled by the Tide Sages, commands the seas with unmatched naval prowess. Home to magical strongholds like Arcanspire and the shadowy Ebonhold Conservatory.
  • Dwargon:
    A neutral dwarven kingdom, famed for its master craftsmanship and ruled by the steadfast King Hjoldar III. Its people prefer to remain isolated, focusing on perfecting their legendary trade skills.
  • Citeri Accords:
    This is the homeland of the wood elves and other woodland races, united by the Citari Accords after the brutal War of Ashes. Peaceful but wary, they live in harmony with nature and magic.
  • Bear Island:
    A remote land inhabited by the Ursarin, sentient bear-like beings. Their ancient traditions keep them isolated from most of the world, except when they embark on their mysterious pilgrimages.
  • Isles of Lhantos:
    A dragon-touched island nation ruled by Queen Aurelia, shrouded in mystery and often independent of the mainland's politics. Humans and dragonborn make up most of its population.
  • Seventh Aristocracy of Zrina:
    This coastal land, known for honey and its scholarly Olluwak Keepers, is a haven for historians and intellectuals. The noble Eladrin Elves and Loxodon hold power here.
  • Ivory Islands:
    A jungle archipelago inhabited by Loxodon, fiercely protected in exchange for their ivory by the nobles of Zrina. A land of dense forests and secluded tribes.
  • Thmora:
    A desert nation with its capital, Ethos, serving as a bustling trade hub. Its people are resilient, with Earth Genasi, Duergar, and Lizardfolk dominating the arid landscapes.
  • Deepholme:
    A fiery volcanic city-state home to the Obsidian Dwarves and Fire Genasi. Known for its obsidian walls and defensive strategies, it stands firm against natural and political threats.
  • Droźag Oligarchy:
    A small, ethically questionable nation known for its medical advancements. It is home to secretive Void Elves and rare Simic Hybrids, pushing the boundaries of science and magic.
  • The Borderlands:
    A peaceful neutral territory, its fertile lands to the south and barren wastes to the north form a bridge between the northern and southern nations. It is a refuge for many races seeking a simple life.
  • Magocracy of Vehlnhiem:
    An arctic realm where power is determined by arcane ability, ruled by a Wizard King. Citizens strive for magical supremacy, with Air Genasi and other powerful magic-users in abundance.
  • Trese’ Dynasty:
    A reclusive, human-only nation known for its unmatched swordsmanship and isolationist policies. Outsiders are rarely welcomed in this land of tradition and honor.
  • Northern Union of Nordenya:
    A confederation of independent feudal lords who have opened their land to trade, uniting their snowy domain under a banner of mutual economic benefit.
  • Sylvarian Diarchy:
    A realm governed by two rulers: Cygaros Frostblade of the Snow Elves and human King Gavin Jehlur Rosengad. Though historically hostile to Fithe, it thrives on its multicultural citizenry.
  • New Elzhora:
    A city-state dominated by warforged, blending magic and technology in a society focused on TechnoArcana. The warforged control the city, with very few organic beings in power.
  • Republic of Rezevera:
    A diverse and fast-growing nation, Rezevera is known for its mining wealth and rapid modernization under the leadership of Rytari Nythameen. Various races thrive here in a democratic society.
